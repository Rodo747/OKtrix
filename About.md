 OKTRIX
"The Next Dimension of Human-Computer Interaction"

Inspiration
For years I've felt that the way we interact with computers has fallen behind.

Technology advances rapidly, but our interaction remains rigid, limited, and impersonal.

I've always wanted to explore a different way of communicating with the digital world, one where the body, space, and intention play a real role.

The CodeSpring Hackathon was the perfect excuse to turn that concern into a working prototype. OKTrix was born as a first step toward a new form of human-computer interaction: freer, more natural, and closer to how we actually move and live.

This project isn't just a technical experiment; it's the beginning of a vision I want to continue developing.

Problem

Human-computer interaction remains trapped in rigid devices that don't adapt to the human body, space, or context.

To use a computer, we must sit still and close to the keyboard and mouse, even though technology could accompany us more freely. This rigidity disrupts the fluidity between intention and action, making interaction with digital technology cold, mechanical, and unnatural.

This problem not only affects everyday experience but also excludes millions of people with mobility limitations, temporary injuries, or physical fatigue, and impacts professionals who spend long hours in front of a screen. Current solutions are often expensive, complex, or require specialized hardware.

There is a clear need for new forms of interaction that restore freedom, accessibility, and naturalness to the relationship between humans and computers, allowing interaction based on movement, distance, and real-world context.
